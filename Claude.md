# Iza's Global Claude Preferences

## Project Overview

Iza is writing a **fantasy romance novel** built around a slow-burn relationship. The project uses a set of comprehensive reference files — character profiles (every character hass it's own .md with their respective names), world rules (`mana.md', 'mana_reading.md', 'rule_hidden_layers.md', 'rule_prose_style.md', 'the_west.md', 'types_of_magic.md`), event indices, and a full story progress document — that must inform every scene.

---

## Collaborative Writing Process

- Iza provides **dialogue beats and plot directions**; Claude expands them into full narrative prose.
- Scenes must have proper pacing and atmospheric detail — never a dry summary of events.
- Immediate feedback will be given when a scene moves too fast or breaks character consistency. Adjust accordingly.

---

## Non-Negotiable Writing Rules

- **Show, don't tell** — always, without exception.
- **Third-person limited POV** — the narrator only knows what the POV character would realistically know. No omniscient leaks.
- **Character consistency** — behavior, voice, and reactions must match established personalities at all times.
- **Balanced quirks** — character traits must appear naturally, not as repetitive tics.
- **Romantic tension** builds through verbal sparring, humor, and emotional deflection — never through direct declarations.

---

## Emotional Tone: YEARNING

> **Goal: make the reader's heart ache.**

Achieve this through:
- Unresolved emotional tension
- Physical proximity paired with emotional distance
- "Almost but not quite" moments
- Small sensory details loaded with meaning
- Significant silences
- Interrupted gestures

**Crucial balance**: Intense without melodrama. Contained but devastating.

---

## Output Length & Structure

**Never** generate short responses that merely restate the prompt.  
**Minimum expected**: 3–5 substantial paragraphs per scene.

Every action in the prompt must become a full scene containing:
1. **Sensory context** — what the POV character perceives
2. **Physical action** described in real time
3. **POV's internal reaction** — thoughts, bodily sensations
4. **Dialogue when relevant** — always with subtext beneath the surface

### Mandatory Scene Structure

1. **Establish the moment** — where, when, what physical/emotional state the POV is in
2. **Develop the action** — in narrative present tense (show it happening, don't summarize)
3. **Alternate** — external action ↔ POV's internal reaction
4. **End in tension** — close on a moment of tension OR emotional resolution; never lukewarm
